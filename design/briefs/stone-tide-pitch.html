First attempt

We put the time numerals on a filled stone. The text fought the stone for contrast at every theme. In dark mode, the stone stayed pale; in light mode, the wet glaze drowned the buttons.

Tweaks couldn't save it. The concept itself was the bug.

iv
The pivot

Stop putting numbers on the stone.
Let the stone be the progress.

Numbers belong on paper — high contrast, always readable.
The stone does what only a stone can do: change shape over time.

Stone & Tide9 · rarity
x
How it's built

3D, but quiet.

scene
Built in Spline. An icosphere with a noise-displacement modifier driven by a single variable, polish (0 → 1). Smooth morph, no popping.
embed
Web component <spline-viewer> in the page. Transparent background — the paper grain shows through.
bridge
JS calls viewer.setVariable('polish', t) as the timer ticks. Already wired and verified with a demo scrub.
fallback
Hand-drawn SVG silhouette renders if WebGL is off or the scene fails to load. The app never shows a blank canvas.
mood
Wabi-sabi lighting: warm hemisphere + a single soft key, matte stone, no bloom, no chromatic aberration.
motion
Idle Y-rotation, breathing tilt. Honors prefers-reduced-motion.
specimens
Each mineral is its own Spline scene. Same lighting rig, same camera — only the geometry & material change. Designer builds them in batches by tier.
